Brands USA - Paul Artrip scrive per RumClub!

Paul Artrip, alias The Rum Daddy, è un grande intenditore di Rum straconosciuto negli Stati Uniti, sempre presente a conferenze del settore, seminari, festival e in ogni occasione ci sia bisogno del suo panama…
Racconta in esclusiva per RumClub il mercato americano.

When Daniele asked me to write a column about American rums and current trends in the USA, I was honored. Besides Daniele being a great fellow, I am second-generation Italian-American; my family comes from San Marino (Corradi) and Abruzzo (Chiacchieri).

Americans first started distilling rums in the mid-1660s, with a multitude of distilleries popping up throughout the east coast. Molasses was a staple of trade throughout the New World, and easy to ferment. It took the American Revolution in the 18th Century to change trade and imports, resulting in a shortage of molasses; Americans turned to grains and starting making whiskey.

Today, a rising number of microdistillers and artisan distillers are experimenting, creating, and marketing their rum products. Some search to recapture American history, while others note the current trends in changing American tastes. The beer market is either leveling out or dropping, depending upon who talks to you. Cocktails, however, are exploding in popularity, in both traditional and new forms. The American sweet tooth is as strong as ever, and mixology recipes often reflect this fact. The HBO television series ‘Sex And The City’ stimulated an already rising interest in cocktails, and soon bars and restaurants nationwide starting offering their own concoctions. Premium spirits sales are also rising, although lagging behind the mixology products. Flavored rums are selling very well.

Let’s take a tour around the mainland USA for some of the distilleries (we can look at the USVI and Puerto Rico in another column):

NANTUCKET, MASSACHUSETTS Triple 8 Distilleries is named after a famous local water well, #888, which produces spring water of unusual purity. Just what you need for premium distilling! They make an amber/dark Hurricane Rum with a lot of vanilla, sugar, and spices notes.

REHOBETH BEACH, DELAWARE Dogfish Head Brewery is famous for its beers and ales; they now distill through their Dogfish Head Spirits operation. They have a nice line of rums, particularly some of their flavored rums with coconut or citrus notes.

KELSO, TENNESSEE Prichards Distillery is having real success with their line of rums. Matured in small batches, they produce a White Rum with unusual sophistication and depth. Their amber Fine Rum has a spectrum of sugar and toast notes, while their Sweet Georgia Belle and Sweet Lucy are liqueurs based on the distillery rums. The Georgia Belle has peach and mango notes. The Sweet Lucy makes a rum mint julep that defies simple words.

NEW ORLEANS, LOUISIANA Old New Orleans Distillery, located in the heart of the historic American sugar industry, produces their line of rums which includes a very nice and very different Cajun Spiced Rum. It has the expected sweet spices, plus a touch of cayenne (hey, it’s New Orleans!) that sets it apart. I like it neat, no ice. Their Amber Rum is exceptional.

NEWPORT, OREGON Rogue Spirits is producing three rums that I know of, including a really unique Hazelnut Spice Rum that has lots of possibilities for both imbibing and cooking. I not only enjoyed it when sipped, but I also tried using it in a reduction with butter; the resulting sauce matched well with pork as well as desserts when a bit of sugar was added.

MAUI, HAWAII I’m including the Haleakala Distillers in Maui, since Hawaii is way, way far away from the Caribbean. With high quality sugar cane readily available, this distillery makes some premium rums. Their Gold Rum is outstanding. They also have a 155-proof, with the great Hawaiian name of ‘Bruddah Kimos’ Da Bomb’. Gotta love it!

I have regretfully not yet tried the products from GREEN BAY DISTILLER out of Wisconsin, nor the rums from BARDENAY DISTILLERY in Boise, Idaho. There are many more out there which I will endeavor to find this year. If you want more info about American rums, I can recommend a great article by Michael Nagrant of the Chicaco Tribune newspaper, as well as a superb book by the title of ‘And A Bottle Of Rum’ by Wayne Curtis. In addition, Ed Hamilton and his Ministry of Rum website is one of the premier places to learn. Finally, feel free to check out www.therumdaddy.com
Ciao!!
